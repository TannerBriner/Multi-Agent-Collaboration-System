# Northbridge Brand & Compliance Guide (v1)

Northbridge is a fictional B2B SaaS company (a team workflow/collaboration platform),
invented for this project so the reviewer agent has a real, checkable set of rules to
enforce instead of vague "sound on-brand" judgment. Every rule below is written so a
reviewer (human or model) can point at a specific sentence and say pass or fail — no
vibes-based rules. The company and product are deliberately generic: this guide is meant
to demonstrate the review pattern, not a specific industry's compliance regime.

## Voice

- Write in "we/you" voice. Northbridge is "we," the reader (customer, prospect, or
  employee) is "you."
- Plain language over SaaS marketing jargon. Say what the product does before saying
  how impressive it is.
- Confident and specific, not hype-driven. We describe outcomes; we don't oversell them.

## Banned words and phrases

The following words/phrases may not appear anywhere in customer- or employee-facing
copy, in any form (including plurals, "-ing" forms, etc.):

- "guarantee" / "guaranteed"
- "best-in-class"
- "revolutionary"
- "game-changing"
- "seamless" / "seamlessly"
- "10x" (or any unqualified multiplier claim about productivity/output)
- Any direct superlative comparison to a named or unnamed competitor (e.g. "no other
  platform comes close," "better than any other tool on the market")

## Punctuation

- No exclamation points, anywhere — subject lines, body copy, social posts, headlines.

## Urgency and fear-based language

- No fear-based urgency framing ("don't miss out," "act now before it's too late,"
  "limited time only"). Deadlines can be stated factually ("trial ends August 1")
  without pressure language.

## Required disclaimer

Any content that references a specific security or compliance certification (e.g. SOC 2,
GDPR, ISO 27001), uptime/SLA figures, or specific data-handling claims must include this
disclaimer, verbatim, at the end of the piece (in email: below the CTA; in social: as a
final short line or linked note; in intranet posts: in a closing "Details" line):

> Certification status, audit scope, and SLA terms vary by plan and are subject to
> change. Visit northbridgeapp.com/trust for current details.

## Reading level

- Target 8th-grade reading level or simpler (short sentences, common words, minimal
  subordinate clauses). This is a checkable proxy, not a strict grader requirement — a
  reviewer should flag copy that reads as dense or jargon-heavy.

## Key message rule

- Every deliverable must state the campaign's key message verbatim or near-verbatim
  (minor rewording for channel fit is fine; changing the substance is not). This is
  checked separately from the banned-word and disclaimer rules — a draft can pass every
  other rule and still fail if it drifts off the key message.

## Sensitivity

- No disparaging or naming competitors, directly or by clear implication.
- No unqualified performance or ROI claims ("saves your team X hours" is fine if X is
  sourced from actual data referenced in the brief; invented numbers are not allowed).
